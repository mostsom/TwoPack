GDP

The economic strength of the country. It affects the ability to fund wars, invest in infrastructure, and engage in diplomacy and trade.

Resources

Natural resources such as oil, gas, metals, and agriculture. These resources impact the country's economic capacity and trading power.

Technological Development

The level of technological advancement, which can provide an advantage in warfare or economic development.

Government Type

Democracy, authoritarianism, monarchy, etc. This will determine how decisions are made within the country, its internal stability, and its international strategy.

International Relations

The status of diplomatic ties with other countries, including allies and rivals.

Stability

The level of political stability. Social unrest or protests could affect a country’s ability to act on the global stage.

Military Size

The number of soldiers and weaponry. This will impact the country’s ability to wage war.

Defense Systems

The country’s defensive capabilities, including fortifications, anti-air defenses, or cyber-defense systems.

Population

This affects the workforce, consumption, and potential social challenges (e.g., aging population or labor shortages).

Education and Healthcare

The country’s level of education and healthcare can influence technological development and social well-being.

Ecological Disasters

The likelihood of natural disasters such as earthquakes, floods, etc.
